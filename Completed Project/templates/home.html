Common Phishing Techniques

Email Phishing

Cybercriminals send deceptive emails impersonating legitimate companies to steal personal information.

Website Spoofing

Fake websites that mimic legitimate ones to capture login credentials and personal data.

SMS Phishing

Text messages that trick users into clicking malicious links or sharing sensitive information.

Warning Signs to Watch For

From: security@arnaz0n.com

Account Security Alert

Dear Valued Customer,

We've detected unusual activity on your account. Please verify your identity immediately by clicking the link below:

http://security-verify.arnaz0n.com/account

Suspicious Domain

Notice the misspelled domain "arnaz0n" instead of "amazon"

Urgent Action Required

Creating a sense of urgency is a common phishing tactic

Generic Greeting

Legitimate companies usually use your actual name

Prevention Strategies

Use Strong Passwords

Create unique, complex passwords for all accounts and enable two-factor authentication.

Verify URLs

Always check website addresses carefully and look for secure HTTPS connections.

Check Email Headers

Examine sender addresses and email headers for signs of spoofing.

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your software and security tools to protect against vulnerabilities.

Can You Spot the Phish?

Test your skills with real-world examples and interactive challenges

  • Example 1: Payment Service Alert

    From: service@paypai.com

    Your account has been limited

    Dear Customer,

    We've detected suspicious login attempts. Verify your account now to avoid permanent limitation:

    https://paypai.com/secure-login

    What's Wrong Here?

    • Misspelled domain (paypai vs paypal)
    • Generic greeting instead of your name
    • Urgent threat to force quick action
    • Link doesn't match official PayPal domain

  • Example 2: Bank Login Page

    https://secure.chase-bank.com.verify-account.net

    Chase Online Banking

    Red Flags:

    • Actual domain is "verify-account.net" (not chase.com)
    • No padlock icon in address bar
    • Asks for full credentials upfront
